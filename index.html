Krushna Dash

I am a junior studying Computational Engineering and Mathematics at UT Austin, working primarily on AI research and tooling. My current role is at the Center for Space Research as an ML Research Assistant.

My work spans probabilistic and representation learning, including deep state-space models for motion forecasting, simulation-based modeling of nonlinear systems, and embedding-based semantic search. This work spans the full ML pipeline, from data modeling to evaluation and deployment, across both research-driven and product-oriented projects. Current interests include scalable learning systems, evaluation and robustness, RL-adjacent methods for decision-making and control, and learning for robotics grounded in real-world dynamics.
