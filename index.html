Krushna Dash

I am a junior studying Computational Engineering and Mathematics at UT Austin, working primarily on AI research and tooling. I am a machine learning researcher at the Center for Space Research, working on uncertainty-aware modeling and data-driven methods for complex dynamical systems.

My experience spans probabilistic machine learning, representation learning, and applied ML systems, with projects including deep state-space models for motion forecasting, simulation-based modeling of nonlinear systems, and embedding-based semantic search. I have worked across the full ML pipeline—from data preprocessing and modeling to evaluation and deployment—on both research-driven and product-oriented projects. My interests include scalable learning systems, model evaluation and robustness, reinforcement learning–adjacent methods, and building open, interpretable ML systems that bridge theory and real-world use.
